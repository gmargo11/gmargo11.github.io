Gabriel Margolis

I am a PhD student at the Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL) at MIT, where I work on Embodied Intelligence. Advised by Pulkit Agrawal, I study learned control as a component of complete robotic systems. Previously, I received my BS ('20) and MEng ('21) degrees at MIT.

Email  /  Google Scholar  /  Twitter  /  GitHub

My goal is to advance reliable and helpful mobile robots for assistance, delivery, emergency response, and sport. I'm very interested in the following topics:

  • Agility: How can control algorithms push robots to the limit of their capability rather than conservatively imitating known solutions?
  • Legged Manipulation: What synergies exist between the dynamics of walking and object interaction?
  • Synthetic Data Generation: How can we scalably generate high-quality and diverse motor control data from simulators?
